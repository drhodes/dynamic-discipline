Understanding the Dynamic Discipline

An Adventure in the Foundation of Sequential Digital Logic



Some wires have a little noise,

Some wires have a lot!

Combinational devices use the static discipline to mitigate noise.

Sequential devices use the dynamic discipline to mitigate glitches around clock edges.

The plan is to use feedback with a lenient mux to build a reliable D-latch.

Here are the steps outlined in the sequential logic section of the computation structures notes

To reliably latch 1

Apply 1 to D, holding G=1

After another TPD, Q’ & D both valid for $t_{PD}$; will hold Q=1 independently of G

Set G=0, while Q’ & D hold Q=D

After TPD, 1 appears at Q=Q’

After another TPD, G=0 and Q’ are sufficient to hold Q=1 independently of D
