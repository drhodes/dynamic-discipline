Understanding the Dynamic Discipline



Some wires have a little noise,

Some wires have a lot!

Combinational devices use the static discipline to mitigate noise.

Sequential devices use the dynamic discipline to mitigate glitches around clock edges.

The plan is to use feedback with a lenient mux to build a reliable D-latch.

Here are the steps outlined in the sequential logic section of the computation structures notes

A MUX WARM UP

Control the mux by sampling the waveforms with your pointer

SLIDE A TRANSITION

Try sliding the transition of the B waveform

To reliably latch $1$ means that changes on $D$ will not affect the $Q$ output

Apply $1$ to $D$, holding $G=1$

After $D$ and $G$ have been valid for $t_{PD}$, the new value $1$ appears at the mux output $Q$ (and hence at the input $Q'$).

Set $G=0$, while $Q’$ and $D$ hold $Q=D$

After $T_{PD}$, $1$ appears at $Q=Q’$

After another $t_{PD}$, $G=0$ and $Q’$ are sufficient to hold $Q=1$ independently of $D$
